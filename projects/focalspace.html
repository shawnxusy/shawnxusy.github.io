AN AR-BASED VIDEO CONFERENCING SYSTEM

Project Overview

FocalSpace introduces a video conferencing system that dynamically recognizes relevant activities and objects through depth sensing and hybrid tracking of multimodal cues, such as voice, gesture, and proximity to surfaces. FocalSpace uses this information to enhance usersâ€™ focus by diminishing the background through synthetic blur effects.

Date: February 2012
Duration: 3 months
Role: Interaction design, Kinect development

Step #1. Design system layout

We decided to use both Kinects and Webcams to achieve the desired effect. The first step was to roughly sketch out how the overall system should work.

  • Kinects are used to interpret depth, detecting skeletons, also tracking audio.

  • Webcams are used to detect activities, and present HD images when necessary

  • What is transferred is an augmented video with focus and attention

  • Initial overall system design

Step #2. Separate system into layers

FocalSpace is a rather complicated system. Once we have the idea and overall design, we started breaking down the system to different layers for separate implementation.

Decomposing the system into four layers

Step #3. Implementing core features
After system breakdown, we implemented the main features of FocalSpace, mostly working with depth and audio data from Kinect.

  • Extracting body contour from Kinect

  • Zoom-in view for flipchart and tablets

  • Focused object is streamed to mobile devices in real time

Project Summary

By observing the space we inhabit as a richly layered, semantic object, FocalSpace can be valuable tool for other applications domains beyond video conferencing system. For future work, we consider adding eye-tracking as an additional feature, where focusing follows a user's gaze.
